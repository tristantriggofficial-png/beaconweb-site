My Story

From web design to business automation.

I started BeaconWeb building websites for local service businesses in the Coachella Valley. Custom code, fast load times, SEO — the works. And the sites performed. The problem was, I kept watching clients spend money on ads and websites while losing a huge percentage of their leads before they ever had a conversation.

An auto repair shop would get 20 inquiries in a week. The owner would call 12 of them back the next morning. Six had already booked somewhere else. That's not a marketing problem — the ads worked. It's a response time problem. The jobs were there. The speed wasn't.

I started building automation to fix response time. The results were immediate. One client went from a 40% contact rate to over 80% in the first month — just by having an automated text go out within 60 seconds of every missed call and form submission.

That's when the focus shifted. Now I build one thing — the Lead-to-Appointment System — specifically for automotive businesses where speed-to-lead is the single biggest factor in whether you get the job.

While we specialize in automotive, this system works for any business where response speed determines whether you get the job.

The Pivot

Why I stopped leading with websites.

Websites matter. SEO matters. But neither one helps you if your response time is hours instead of seconds.

Website & SEO — still relevant

Your website is the front door to your automation. SEO brings the leads in. But once someone submits a form or calls and misses — that's where the system takes over. A great website without fast follow-up is just a pretty brochure.

🤖
Automation — where the money is

The highest-leverage thing a service business can do is respond faster and follow up longer. Not run more ads. Not redesign their logo. Respond faster. The system I build does that — and keeps doing it at 2am on a Saturday.
